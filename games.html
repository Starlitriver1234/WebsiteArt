Judge Mental

My role in development: Writer, Artist, Creative Lead

Judge Mental is a comedic visual novel in which you play as a judge and must determine whether the person in front of you is guilty.

You play as Judge Orion and you have one task! Get to know and judge the criminal in front of you. But be careful, making the right choice is harder than it looks when the information you are given can be deceiving.

Enjoy the story of this comedic visual novel with a focus on zany humour and unexpected consequences. Interact with Andrew and get to know him in order to determine if he is innocent or guilty. And of course, have a great time!

You will be presented with options after each dialogue. Choose the one that you think is right and see which ending you get! There are 4 endings in total. Good luck!

This game took 4 months to make and was made in a team of 3 in Unity. I worked as the Creative Lead, the writer and the artist of the game. The first prototype was made by me in Twine. It was made to see how the story would be structured, and also to give an idea on how the game should play. The dialogue of the game was written by me and has a comedic and chaotic feel. I tried to give the players the opportunity to be as chaotic as possible. So there are many weird choices that they can choose. From guillotine sentences to romance options. My goal for this game was to make a satirical game that was absolutely insane. And I am proud to say that me and my team fully succeeded at that.

The artwork of the game, the icons, the logo, the backgrounds and the characters were made by me in Procreate. I wanted the game to have a charming and simple art style that was very expressive and cartoonish. Because of this, the expressions of Andrew are exaggerated, which helps the comedic tone. I started with a finished version of the Andrew artwork and a sketch of the court room. Over time, more drawings and expressions were created for Andrew, as well as a finished version of the background image. Images for each ending were also made. The game was originally going to be bigger, but I noticed that we wouldn’t have time to make more cases and also focus on quality. So I took the decision to scrap the other cases and focus the team’s attention on the quality of the main case instead. This resulted in a way better game that runs better, has a bigger case than the original vision and more endings.

I am very proud of how this game came out, and I learned a lot while making it. I learned how to make better artwork for video games. I learned how to be a leader and when to cut content for the betterment of the project. And I learned how to work better in a team.

The Sea of Loss and Sorrow

My role in development: Writer, Creative Lead, Programmer

This game was made for a university assignment and is a Poetry game. The game is about the exploration of overthinking and how it can drown us in our own thoughts.

It is a very simple game that focuses on atmosphere. Because of that, I put a lot of effort in the Unity screen effects to make it feel like you are under the water. I also used a song composed by me and edited it to sound more eerie. The game was based on the following poem that I wrote and was inspired by a visual I always had when I thought of anxiety. Sinking in a dark sea.

Even in the darkest depths.

Even in the darkest times.

There is a light.

A light that guides.

It's our own hope.

And it never dies.

And once we embrace that.

It all rhymes.
